The opportunities of Digital Simulations and Serious Gaming

Computer technology allows for more efficient, safer and satisfying ways of education. Such methods of Computer technologies in present education is through Digital Simulations and Serious Gaming, which both leave opportunities to take education onto the next level.

Thanks to the advances that have been made in computers and technology, in modern educational institutions, students are now gaining and training skills in totally different ways compared to decades, centuries ago. Almost everything that exists in the world can be embodied in simulations and we can practice our studies and train whenever, and wherever. Students in the past used to study with paper textbooks, and not only did they have fewer chances to learn their interested fields whenever they wanted, but they also had fewer opportunities to interact with computers and technology for study in schools and universities. However, students these days are living in a world that cannot live without computers even in their studies. Computers are now widely used around the world, and computer-based digital simulation and serial gaming have brought numerous positive effects and provided more opportunities to students in terms of practicing skills and acquiring skills more easily and more fun. This paragraph will investigate four different types of opportunities for society by using digital simulations and serious gaming based on our research.

Firstly, virtual environments and simulations can offer a safer alternative to the real world. They provide controlled experimentation and increased accessibility examples include medical training, product design, aviation, military training, and entertainment. As technology advances, virtual environments and simulations are becoming more accurate that shows outstanding similarity to the real world, widespread, offering new possibilities for various fields. Jenn (2021) states that simulations are harmless places where the mistakes of simulation users are allowed and have no effect. This implies that digital simulations are safety friendly programmes with significant potentials that will be even more commonly used in the future education.

Secondly, digital simulations allow people such as designers and engineers to test and refine designs and models before committing to physical prototypes. Working on big projects such as building structures or developing machines require precise and accurate design and error free structures because minor mistakes might cause irreparable disasters. By implementing and using digital simulations, designers and developers can also model and test complex systems, identifying and addressing potential issues early on. This reduces costs and improves effectiveness. Such simulation models include 3D modeling, which offers insight and greater understanding to solve problems and make decisions through a project’s visualization (Cedreo, 2023).

The ability to explore "what if…" questions in digital simulations and serious gaming offers another opportunity. It not only allows users to practice in safer ways, evaluate strategies, but also enables the exploration of alternative methods, fosters creativity and critical thinking. By training with serious gaming, users become more confident of what they are doing and ultimately perform their best when the real situation happens. Serious gaming gives users more confidence in what they do, and ultimately leads them to perform their best when they actually encounter real situations. As well as improvements to cognition, both digital simulations and serious gaming can improve physical efficiency. According to Keeling (2023), approximately more than one quarter of the surgeons who have practiced with serious gaming showed superior surgical perfomance with fewer mistakes than surgeons without serious gaming experiences. Overall, it provides a dynamic environment for learning, experimentation and analysis. This shows that serious gaming is becoming one of the most powerful and irreplaceable training approaches in many fields.

Lastly, the ability to control time in digital simulations and serious gaming offers advantages such as accelerated learning, rapid iteration, and enhanced analysis. It allows users to observe long-term effects in a shorter time frame and make real-time decisions. It facilitates training and skill development and helps visualize changes in complex systems. There are little to no consequences in making mistakes during a simulation in comparison to its real-world counterpart (Keeling, 2023).
