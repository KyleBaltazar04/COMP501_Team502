Topic Definition

The idea of using digital simulations and serious aming hasn’t always been something many can agree on when it comes to education, as many see it as a way for people to waste time and effort on a virtual screen while neglecting their peers, becoming lazy and being less social. However, over the past few years, many teachers and students have come to realize how beneficial such an opportunity it is to use these skills for their studies and work.

The use of computer technologies for educational purposes has increased over time, ultimately leading to educational techniques such as ‘Serious Gaming’ and Digital Simulations. To generate extrinsic value, simulation gaming, often known as serious gaming, blends game technology and play concepts. It encourages participation, offers criticism, and supports learning and growth. Benefits include more in-depth instruction, realistic case studies, participation, quick feedback, group learning, affordable training, and ongoing development. Digital Simulations and Serious Gaming are both new approaches to teaching and practising using computer technologies and some people might think that they sound the same, however, there are two different purposes and focuses. Serious gaming is not for entertainment purposes, but an educational method that provides engaging, dynamic activities that foster students to learn and study in a more fun and thought-provoking way (Weber-Sabil et al., 2021). Basically, are intended digital games for educational, practical, and training purposes that help and stimulate the users to develop problem-solving and decision-making skills. It is mainly used in education and training fields aiming for users to achieve their goals in interesting ways. SimCity could be an example of a serious game that gives the opportunity to act like the mayor of a city and users can plan and actually create a fully functioning city with different areas such as financial, industrial complex and residential areas (Baecker, 2019). On the other hand, Digital Simulations can be defined as computer simulations that imitate real-world systems and reproduce particular scenarios and problems in artificial and virtual environments (Baek, 2009). The major goal is to help and guide users to learn and develop their skills in safer, cost-efficient ways. For instance, flight simulators, car crash modelling, and climate predictions are well-known examples.
