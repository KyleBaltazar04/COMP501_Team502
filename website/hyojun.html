Ethical Issues

Advances in computer technology and the implementation of digital simulation and serious gaming in education have brought obvious benefits, but at the same time, it is also causing ethical issues. This paragraph is going to state two outstanding ethical issues.

The first issue is the less accessibility to disabled people. Many digital simulations are developed based on users without disabilities, which implies that there will be limitations for people with disabilities to use. In this case, it might be an ethical issue since it shows unfairness among the users because some groups of people are excluded from its use. For example, individuals with mobility impairments might find themselves uncomfortable with standard computer desks that are lower than their wheelchairs, while those with blindness would find difficulties when accessing visual materials (Burgstahler, 2022).

To minimize this issue, a lot of effort and attention is needed from the beginning of the development so that there are no inconveniences and limitations even for disabled people. Furthermore, it is necessary to ensure that everyone can utilize and learn new skills through continuous developments and new version releases. Humans should be always treated fairly and not be discriminated against for any reason.

Another ethical issue is the reduction in social and physical interactions. Nowadays, there are numerous numbers of advantages and opportunities to implement computers in training and learning such as safety, time and space-free environments, and allowance for users to learn better through trials and errors without any penalties caused by mistakes. However, no matter how high the accuracy level of the simulators is made to reproduce the real world as much as possible, it still lacks the tension and human interaction that the users could feel in real situations. According to Bidwell (2019), as screen time increases, it not only decreases human interaction, physical activity and socializing skills but also results in issues of obesity in the country.

It is undeniable that modern technology helps students and users to develop their skills, however, it could also make people isolated from society, who improperly use this technology.

Self-Reflection

As a university student, I use computers every day, including digital simulations and serious gaming in my courses. I have found both advantages and disadvantages to using these technologies. For example, in my Networks and Internet course, I used the Packet Tracer program, which helps us to better understand how the network works and gives us opportunities to build a network and configure the devices from scratch. Even if I make mistakes while installing new devices and changing settings on devices, there are no penalties. However, as I stated above, there are ethical issues that can arise in our daily lives and in our education as well.

People who have visual impairments may find it hard to interact with computer visual interfaces. To address this issue, simulation developers can make contents bigger, apply vocal and audio mechanisms for assistance, and seek feedback from current users. I now realize that some students may find it difficult to use those simulations and serious gaming, and they may even experience negative side effects. Therefore, whenever I am implementing or using those new technologies in education in the future, I will also think about how to improve those negative aspects in ways that everyone can enjoy and equally participate in, ultimately helping students to enhance their skills without risks. In addition, since excessive screen time can negatively impact young students, if I ever get a chance to teach young students, I will try to minimize the use of digital resources and instead encourage them to interact with physical resources such as paper-based books. Moreover, I will encourage them to spend more time outdoors rather than spending time in front of screens.
