Ethical Issues

With the many benefits that computers bring into education, it also comes with its own ethical issues. An issue which stems from education through computer technology is the dehumanization of education. A key aspect of education factors in having a social relationship between the teacher and student. Using computers in education discourages social activity inside classrooms, which can remove this social aspect almost entirely. The dehumanization of education is an important ethical issue which must be addressed as it can deteriorate or lose both human and social values. With computers being more involved in education, the communication between the student and teacher becomes more obscure, deteriorating the social values of humans (Allwood, 2017). This issue can be resolved if both digital simulation and serious games are used as a tool to help teachers educate students, so that teachers can still interact with students, which can implicitly teach human and social values.

Another issue from both serious games and digital simulations is its high cost to apply it in education. Educational institutes who are provided with less money for their education will find it difficult to teach in real-world situations compared to more wealthy educational institutes. This ethical issue results in unfairness, as not all students will receive the same amount of high-level education and awareness in real-world situations. It is estimated to have a price range between $10,000 to $100,000 to enroll students in digital simulation training or purchase simulations for construction training (Carter, 2018). The obvious issue to resolve this problem is to reduce the cost of both serious games and digital simulations, but it will be difficult to find the right price range due to expensive resources to create them both.

Reflection

I find the topic of both digital simulations and serious games useful for education, and speaking as a student, I will look forward to ways on how much of an impact both digital simulations and serious games can make in my learning process. Reflecting on the opportunities, risks and choices that we’ve researched, I’ve evaluated positive and negative aspects for if I were to use digital simulations and serious games in my education.

A positive for using digital simulations and serious games is how effective it is in making learning an efficient process. Using digital simulations is an effective way to design realistic creations within a virtual space, as well as easily test designs without the need to physically replicate. This is a positive as it helps reduce time spent on learning or experimenting, as well as reducing the number of resources required by a large margin. From personal experience, using digital simulations has greatly decreased the amount of time spent. Integrating digital simulations and serious games will help me learn in a suitable span of time, which is especially helpful as I get busier overtime in my education.

A negative which comes from digital simulations and serious games is its high cost. Based on the risks that come with digital simulations and serious games, I also feel as if I will not be able to take digital simulations and serious games as seriously as real-world alternatives. Because the two methods of education are performed online, I have less consequences for performing poorly in my learning, therefore, I am unable to learn as well as in the real-world. The high cost will also be detrimental, especially since as a college student, having digital simulations and serious games integrated into my education will leave more to my expense.
